**Deep Learning based Predictive Crowd Stampede
Analysis and Rerouting**

Crowd stampedes remain one of the most
devastating and unpredictable events in densely populated
gatherings. Sudden panic or localized congestion can rapidly
escalate into fatal incidents. This study proposes a deep
learning–based predictive framework for early detection and
prevention of crowd stampedes through intelligent behavioral
analysis and rerouting. The framework integrates a hybrid
detection architecture combining YOLOv8 and DETR for robust,
occlusion-resilient localization, followed by spatio-temporal
modeling using Farneback optical flow, ConvLSTM, and LSTM
networks to capture dynamic motion patterns. In parallel, a
Graph Convolutional Network (GCN) is employed to model
inter-personal interactions and collective behavioral cues.
Predictions from these modules are fused to generate a
high-confidence risk assessment of potential crowd congestion or
instability. The primary aim of this research is to establish a
reliable and adaptive predictive system capable of identifying
high-risk crowd behavior and facilitating timely rerouting
interventions. Key challenges addressed include maintaining
detection accuracy under severe occlusion, modeling complex
group dynamics, and ensuring the framework’s generalizability
across varied real-world crowd settings.
